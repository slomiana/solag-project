The Spring of Life School (SOLAG) is in the Sinchu Alagie area of the Gambia and was founded in 2015 by three friends Joseph Mendy, Andrew Mendy and Paul Gomez.Their aim was to give children who would not otherwise be able to go to school a chance to access an education and in their words “to narrow the gap between the have and the have nots and help give them a springboard into life”.

There are currently 305 students in the school which caters for Nursery to Grade 6 which is a full primary school cycle in the Gambia.About 100 boys and girls take part at the school football project – Onside United. The area around the school is very poor and the school itself has very few resources.

Apart from providing education to the children, SOLAG provides workshops campaigns in local communities against Child marriages and Female Genital Mutilation (FGM).

Children are given opportunities to develop in many areas such as arts, science and sports.

During COVID lockdown we paid teachers’ salaries and have continued to support the school through the provision of much-needed school resources and equipment from tables and chairs to reading books, school bags, textbooks, paints and pencils etc.

The school is curre ntly looking for new premises as the landlord has given a notice to leave.At present half the schools are having lessons on the old site while half the schools are having lessons in a new building which is being rented. Unless the school can raise the money to purchase the new building they will have to move again.
